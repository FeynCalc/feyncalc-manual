\section{Convolute}\label{convolute}}

\texttt{Convolute[\allowbreak{}f,\ \allowbreak{}g,\ \allowbreak{}x]}
convolutes \(f(x)\) and \(g(x)\), i.e.,
\(\int _0^1 dx_1 \int _0^1 dx_2 \delta \left(x - x_1 x_2\right) f (x_1) g(x_2)\).

\texttt{Convolute[\allowbreak{}f,\ \allowbreak{}g]} is equivalent to
\texttt{Convolute[\allowbreak{}f,\ \allowbreak{}g,\ \allowbreak{}x]}.

\texttt{Convolute[\allowbreak{}exp,\ \allowbreak{}\{\allowbreak{}x1,\ \allowbreak{}x2\}]}
assumes that \texttt{exp} is polynomial in \texttt{x1} and \texttt{x2}.
Convolute uses table-look-up and does not do any integral calculations,
only linear algebra.
